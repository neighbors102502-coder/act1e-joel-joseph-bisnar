Baybayin: The Living Script

Baybayin is an alpha-syllabary writing system where each character represents a consonant-vowel combination. It was traditionally etched onto bamboo slats or heavy leaves using a sharp point.

The most significant artifact of this era is the Laguna Copperplate Inscription (900 AD). This document proves the existence of a complex legal and financial system in the Philippines long before European contact.

Social Hierarchy

Pre-colonial society was a highly stratified system led by a Datu or Rajah. The Maginoo formed the ruling royalty, while the Timawa were the free men and respected commoners.

The Maharlika served as the elite warrior class. At the base were the Alipin, who were individuals in debt or servitude but maintained specific rights, unlike the western concept of chattel slavery.

The Balangay Builders

Early Filipinos were renowned "People of the Sea." The Balangay was a sophisticated wooden boat built without nails, using wooden pegs and fiber lashings to hold the planks together.

These vessels allowed the kingdoms of Butuan and Sulu to dominate maritime trade. They exchanged gold and pearls for silk and porcelain along the maritime Silk Road.

The word Barangay is actually derived from Balangay, signifying a community that traveled together on a single boat.
