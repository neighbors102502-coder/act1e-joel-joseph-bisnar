The Cry of Rebellion

The Kataastaasan, Kagalanggalangang Katipunan ng mga Anak ng Bayan (KKK) was a revolutionary movement that sought total independence from Spain. Unlike previous localized revolts, the Katipunan united Filipinos across different provinces under a single cause: Kalayaan.

The revolution officially began with the "Cry of Pugad Lawin," where members tore up their cedulas (tax certificates) to signify their defiance against Spanish authority. This act sparked a nationwide conflict that eventually crippled Spanish military control over the archipelago.

June 12, 1898

On June 12, 1898, General Emilio Aguinaldo proclaimed Philippine independence from the balcony of his home in Kawit, Cavite. For the first time, the Philippine National Flag was unfurled, and the National Anthem was played in public.

This led to the Malolos Congress in 1899, which produced the first democratic constitution in Asia. It established the First Philippine Republic, proving to the world that Filipinos were capable of governing themselves as a modern, sovereign nation.

The Bitter Struggle

The dream of independence was challenged by the arrival of the United States. The Philippine-American War was a brutal conflict characterized by guerrilla warfare and the heroic last stands of Filipino generals like Gregorio del Pilar at Tirad Pass.

Despite being outgunned by the superior technology of the American forces, the Filipino revolutionaries fought for over three years. While the Republic eventually fell, the spirit of the revolution laid the groundwork for the future pursuit of liberty through diplomatic means.

Historical Fact: The Malolos Constitution was written in Spanish, as it was the language of the educated class (Ilustrados) who led the legal transition to independence.
