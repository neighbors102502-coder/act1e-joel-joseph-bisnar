Leptospirosis

Leptospirosis is a disease caused by the bacterium Leptospira that can be found in contaminated water or soil. It affects many different kinds of animals and people. Without treatment, leptospirosis in people can lead to kidney damage, meningitis (inflammation of the membrane around the brain and spinal cord), liver failure, trouble breathing, and even death.

Leptospirosis cases in the Philippines have seen a significant, concerning increase, particularly following monsoon-related flooding in 2025. As of mid-2025, cases have risen over 43% compared to 2024, with severe outbreaks occurring in Metro Manila and Western Visayas, primarily affecting males exposed to contaminated floodwaters.

Cholera

Cholera is an acute diarrheal infection caused by ingesting food or water contaminated with Vibrio cholerae bacteria, often leading to severe, rapid dehydration and death within hours if untreated. Key symptoms include sudden, severe watery diarrhea ("rice-water stools") and vomiting. Treatment focuses on immediate rehydration using oral rehydration salts (ORS) or intravenous fluids, with antibiotics for severe cases. The 1882 cholera outbreak in the Philippines was a devastating epidemic, especially in Manila and surrounding provinces, causing an estimated 15,000 to 30,000 deaths. Originating from the steamer Eolus in early 1882, the disease spread from Zamboanga to Panay and Manila, leading to high mortality rates, the closure of schools like the University of Santo Tomas, and significant social disruption.

Coronavirus

Coronavirus disease (COVID-19) is an infectious disease caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus. Most people infected with the virus will experience mild to moderate respiratory illness and recover without requiring special treatment. However, some will become seriously ill and require medical attention.
