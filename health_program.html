National Health Insurance Program

The National Health Insurance Program (NHIP) is run by PhilHealth to make healthcare affordable for Filipinos. All citizens are automatically covered under the Universal Health Care law. It helps pay for hospital care, checkups, and major treatments like dialysis and cancer care.

National Tuberculosis Control Program

The National Tuberculosis Control Program (NTP) is a DOH program in the Philippines that fights tuberculosis. It provides free and effective TB treatment using the DOTS method. It aims to reduce TB cases across the country.

National AIDS, STI Prevention, and Control Program

The NASPCP is a Philippine health program that works to prevent HIV and other STIs. It promotes education, testing, and free treatment for those affected. Its goal is to stop new infections and reduce discrimination.

National Health Insurance Program

The National Health Insurance Program (NHIP) is a Philippine government initiative implemented by PhilHealth to provide affordable, accessible health care for all citizens. Under the Universal Health Care (UHC) Act, all Filipino citizens are automatically included. It operates via case rates, covering inpatient, outpatient, and specialized services like dialysis and cancer treatment to reduce out-of-pocket expenses.

National Tuberculosis Control Program

The National Tuberculosis Control Program (NTP) in the Philippines, managed by the Department of Health (DOH), is a comprehensive public health initiative aimed at reducing the burden of tuberculosis (TB) in the country. Organized in 1978, it operates under the Disease Prevention and Control Bureau and focuses on implementing the Directly Observed Treatment, Short-course (DOTS) strategy to ensure that TB treatment is accessible, free, and effective.

NASPCP - National AIDS, STI Prevention, and Control Program

The National AIDS, STI Prevention, and Control Program (NASPCP) in the Philippines is a comprehensive initiative aiming for zero new HIV infections, discrimination, and deaths. It focuses on education, confidential testing, free antiretroviral therapy (ART) in treatment hubs, and targeted prevention for high-risk populations.
